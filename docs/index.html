Features

⏰

Intrusive Reminders

Full-screen alarm when your phone is locked. Heads-up notification when it's on. You won't miss it.

🔁

Recurring Tasks

Daily, weekly, monthly, yearly — with an optional time of day. Frequency tasks reset automatically after completion.

🔗

Condition-Based Scheduling

Start a task after another finishes, before one starts, or after a named event like "after breakfast".

📋

Nested Tasks

Break any task into subtasks to arbitrary depth. Parents show completion progress across their children.

💬

Open Loops

Waiting on someone? Mark the task as an open loop with a follow-up date. The Loops tab keeps them visible so nothing falls through the cracks.

💤

Snooze

Not right now? Snooze for 5, 10, 15, or 30 minutes. The reminder comes back. You cannot escape your tasks.

🎤

Show Mode

At a gig or event? Enable Show Mode to silence all alarms globally. Switch it off when you're back and reminders resume.

Installing the APK

VDone is not yet on the Google Play Store. Install it directly using the APK file. This requires enabling installs from unknown sources on your device.

  1. Download the APK

    Tap the "Download APK" button above, or go to the Releases page and download vdone.apk.

  2. Allow installs from unknown sources

    On Android 8+: when prompted after opening the APK, tap Settings and enable "Allow from this source" for your browser or file manager. On older Android: go to Settings → Security → Unknown sources and enable it.

  3. Open the APK and install

    Open the downloaded file from your notifications or the Downloads folder. Tap Install and wait for it to complete.

  4. Grant permissions on first launch

    VDone will ask for Notifications, Exact alarms, and Display over other apps (full-screen intents). All three are needed for reminders to work reliably.

About this app

VDone is local only. All your tasks stay on your device. No account required. No data leaves your phone. No analytics, no tracking, no ads.

The code was written by Claude Code. The intent, design, and product decisions were made by a human. Think of it as pair programming where one half of the pair never gets tired.
